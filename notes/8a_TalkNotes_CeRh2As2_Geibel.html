CeRh2As2 talk by Cristoph Geibel

  • New Ce-based heavy-fermion superconductor near Quantum Critical Point (QCP) which might be quadrupolar in nature
  • quadrupolar QCP examples in experiment are very rare and typically Rh based
Structural properties:
  • CaBe2Ge2-type tructure rather than ThCr2Si2-type. This is important because it lack an inversion symmetry at the Ce site
Magnetic properties:
  • Curie-Weiss above 50K
  • Moment close to Ce3+
  • No evidence for order
  • Weak anisotropy
Transport properties:
  • Superconducting below 350mK
  • Kondo scattering evident by hump around 30K-ish in temperature dependence
Heat capacity:
  • Phase transition at 230mK:
    • T1 drops fast with field below 3T
  • Phase transition at 400mK:
    • T2 rises after 6T
  • both T1 and T are highly anisotropic
MuSR experiment proposal has been accepted
