Basics of Magnetism in Ce Systems

Ce3_: one 4f electron
  • Small radius --> strongly localized Moment
  • Large S-L coupling: E_so ~ 250meV ==> Hund's rule for J is important
  • Lowest multiplet is J=5/2 (L=-3, S=1/2)
Non-spherical wave-function:
  • J multiplet is split by Crystal Electric Field (CEF)
  • typically: J is split into 3 doublets
    • Kramers doublet ==> only doublet d.o.f. ==> dipolar ordering
  • cubic: J is split into doublet + quartet
    • quadrupolar ordering
Example of Cubic Ce-systems:
  • CeB6 and Ce3Pd20Si6
  • quadrupolar ordering at T_Q
  • Dipolar ordering at T_N < T_Q
Quadrupolar order parameter difficult to acces
  • T_Q often increases strongly with field (see e.g. [1])
